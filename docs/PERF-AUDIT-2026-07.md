# Performance audit - July 2026

A 43-agent multi-pass audit of the map/camera/threading surface (six area investigators,
every finding adversarially verified against the code before inclusion). 21 findings
confirmed. This file is the tracking doc: the ranked plan below is the synthesis verbatim,
followed by every confirmed finding with its verification verdict.

## Ranked fix list (highest felt-impact-per-risk first)

1. **Fix the overview-to-follow re-attach snap** (VelaMapView.kt ~1054-1095, nav ticker seed block). In the `camState[0].isNaN()` re-seed, also seed `navTiltEase[0] = cp.tilt`, and ease the puck-low padding with a scalar going 0 to 0.45 at the same k instead of baking `height*0.45` on frame one. Reset that scalar in the detached else-branch alongside camState, and make sure it terminates exactly at 0.45 (padding is sticky MapLibre state). Prefer the eased-scalar approach over a one-shot animateCamera, which fights the ticker's per-frame moveCamera. This is the single highest-impact item: it is the literal "route-overview back into nav follow" jolt (one-frame 55-degree tilt snap plus a 22%-of-screen center jump plus a max-velocity zoom ease), reproducible every time.

2. **Stop the nav-detached fall-through to the generic route fit** (VelaMapView.kt ~1830). Use the swallow variant: keep the route-fit branch matched during nav but make its body a no-op when `navMode` (condition `routePolyline.size >= 2 && (navMode || key != lastFittedRouteKey)`), mirroring the existing swallow pattern rather than gating the whole branch on `!navMode` (which exposes the zoom-14.5 else branch one detach earlier). Kills three real bugs in one line-scale change: mid-drive pans yanked to a whole-route flight on the next speedo tick, reroute-while-detached teleports, and the Overview tap's double-flight race with navOverviewTick — the exact "intermittent" hitch signature.

3. **Finish the identity-gate pattern in applyData's tail** (VelaMapView.kt ~3606-3820). Five findings across four areas converged here. Gate: alternates setGeoJson + lineColor (hoist the alternates list into `remember(state.routes, state.activeRoute, state.navigating)` in MapScreen so identity comparison works, matching the `!==` idiom); ME_SRC on (mePaint value, bearing, meStale) with invalidation on navMode/follow transitions (the tickers own the source in their modes — a stale gate freezes the puck); PREVIEW_SRC on preview change with null-transitions still uploading; the browse lineGradient/visibility on a (p, routeInt, trafficSpans, navMode, dashed) key without swallowing the one-shot lastNavRouteMode cleanup; and the four ensure* probes on their booleans. Critically, every new lastApplied holder must be added to the style-reload reset block at ~1684 or theme/palette/satellite flips leave sources invisible. Note the honest sizing from verification: MapLibre's property setters already no-op equal values, so the real win is the setGeoJson churn — dominated by the chooser-open case, where every recomposition (compass ticks, scale-bar steps during the fit flight) re-tessellates up to 4 full alternate polylines mid-flight.

4. **Fix the locate-tap teleport** (VelaMapView.kt ~780-793, free-drive ticker cold-seed). Today any follow re-engagement from zoom<14 is an instant `moveCamera(newLatLngZoom(loc, 15.5))` that also cancels the launch 650ms flight; from zoom>=14 after a pan it eases uncapped multi-km distance with max velocity at t=0. Replace with one animateCamera flight to (fix, 15.5) while a `flyingIn` flag parks the ticker, cleared in BOTH onFinish and onCancel (a leaked flag kills follow; a missing cancel path resurrects the "zoomed to the whole US" crash-relaunch bug this snap was added for), seeding browseCam from the landing camera and tolerating the fix having moved during the flight.

5. **Throttle the compass heading** (MapViewModel.kt:567). Add a min-interval (~150-250 ms sample) alongside the 2-degree gate; keep it in MapUiState. Do NOT reroute it around Compose into a holder — verification showed the free-drive ticker only runs while following, so a holder-only path freezes the browse cone after a pan. This is a multiplier, not a root cause (the heavy uploads are already gated), but it caps browse recompositions from a spiky 5-16/s to ~4-6/s during exactly the pan gestures that stutter, and it makes fix 3's residual per-pass cost fire less often.

6. **Defer ambient partial paints during programmatic flights** (MapViewModel.kt ~4099-4140 + a flag from VelaMapView). Latch the latest FeatureCollection while an animateCamera flight is active and flush on idle with a timeout. Scope it strictly to discrete animateCamera flights — NOT "camera is moving" — because the free-drive follow ticker moveCameras every frame and a generic gate starves ambient paints for entire drives; also flush on cancel (moveCamera cancels flights without a guaranteed idle). This is the mechanism behind dots popping in mid-locate-flight; verification confirmed 3-6 whole-layer placement re-runs land in the seconds after a settle, gated only on job liveness. (It does not touch the nav-follow hitch — ambient is suppressed during nav.)

7. **Gate the nav teardown effect on an actual nav exit** (VelaMapView.kt ~956-977). Track wasNav and run the reset moveCamera only on wasNav && !navMode; keep routePolyline as a key so reroutes still relaunch the puck ticker. Re-apply the current sheet inset directly after the reset rather than poking lastInsetPx = -1 (that re-triggers the grew-check target-null behavior, itself a deliberate fix). Fixes the route-chooser class of hitch: picking an alternate currently kills the fit flight at frame ~0 (the ordering is deterministic, teardown always lands second) and wipes the sheet padding until the inset value next changes.

8. **withMaxZoom(12) on the point GeoJSON sources** (VelaMapView.kt ~2219 and sibling addSource sites; 14 for the accuracy circle polygon; leave ROUTE/ROUTE_AHEAD/line sources alone for lineMetrics/vertex precision). One-liners, essentially zero risk, strictly less geojson-vt re-cutting and placement invalidation per integer zoom during every flight. A minor contributor but the best risk-adjusted line of code in the list.

9. **Bound the nav route-split re-upload** (VelaMapView.kt ~1108-1145). Re-upload only a leading window (2-5 km, zoom-scaled) around the metre-precise cut point every 150 ms; upload the far tail once per route/reroute as a separate feature. Beware: lineGradient line-progress is per-feature, so traffic spans must be re-split across the seam (or the tail gets its own layer), the tail must be refreshed on every reroute/faster-route swap, and the seam must not show at street zoom. Flattens the recurring several-thousand-point tessellation that runs even during the overview flight and re-attach ease.

10. **Make regenRouteDots a single monotonic pass** (VelaMapView.kt ~3356-3397). Keep the segment cursor local to regenRouteDots (d is strictly increasing there) — do not make the shared pointAtMeters stateful, the nav ticker calls it with arbitrary distances. Turns millions of iterations per regen into O(vertices + dots); optionally debounce the 0.2-zoom trigger, accepting slightly laggy dot respacing. Only affects walk/bike routes, but it is ~25 back-to-back main-thread regens plus 3000-feature uploads per pinch when it does.

11. **Skip the speed-limit queryRenderedFeatures poll during flights/gestures** (VelaMapView.kt ~564). Key the skip on discrete animateCamera flights and active gestures, not "camera moving" (nav follow moves every frame — a moving-camera gate starves the badge forever). Do not touch layer lifecycle (the 0.004 lineOpacity is load-bearing). Small periodic main-thread stall, bounded fix.

12. **Optional, minimal only: cache result-bubble bitmaps in a process-wide LRU** keyed (key, dark, satellite) so style reloads and re-searches reuse them; keep rasterization synchronous on main. Skip the off-thread pre-generation entirely — the readiness race renders pins invisible, and the payoff is 1-2 frames on the first search per style. Likewise skip the nav-puck idle-gate finding unless you copy the browse ticker's full recipe (settling flags, padding re-assert, style-reload reseed) — verification showed it is battery waste at a standstill, not frame drops, and a naive gate freezes mid-ease camera state at the first red light.

## Ceiling assessment

With fixes 1-8 applied, a Pixel 9 has the headroom for Google-adjacent smoothness in steady state — camera flights that play at full frame rate, pans that don't micro-hitch from app-side churn. The audit found the architecture is fundamentally sound: correct ease math, correct dispatcher usage, deliberate single-writer guards. Almost everything felt today is self-inflicted timing — style mutations and source uploads landing during the exact windows the render thread is busiest — plus a few camera-ownership seam bugs that read as hitches but are really teleports and cancelled animations.

The structural gap that no amount of app work closes: Google bakes server-ranked POIs and placement priorities into its tiles and places symbols incrementally in C++, so it never pays runtime whole-layer collision re-placement when data arrives. MapLibre re-solves whole-view symbol placement on its single render thread during every camera motion, and every setGeoJson forces a whole-layer re-place. Vela streams POIs at runtime by design (that is the product), so there will always be a placement spike at data-arrival moments in dense areas — deferral (fix 6) moves it to camera-idle where it's invisible, but the spike itself is permanent. Expect: pans and flights indistinguishable from Google most of the time; a brief settle-then-dots-appear rhythm in dense areas where Google's dots are simply already in the tile. That rhythm is the honest ceiling, and it's a good one.

## Are we multithreading the GPU/CPU well?

Yes, with two unavoidable serial chokepoints. The GPU draws everything visible (fills, lines, extrusions, all POI icon/label quads) via GLSurfaceView with no software rendering and no pixel readback on the phone path. CPU parallelism is correctly used: all network fetch + parse on Dispatchers.IO with a parallel async/awaitAll fan-out, ranking on Dispatchers.Default, and MapLibre's own C++ worker pool parallelizes tile decode and tessellation. The heavy one-shot work (applyLight/applyDark's ~50 setProperties) is confined to style-load callbacks, not per-frame.

The two genuinely single-threaded resources are (a) MapLibre's render thread, which serializes symbol collision placement (re-run during every camera motion), every setGeoJson/setProperties this codebase issues, and drawing itself; and (b) the main thread, which hosts both withFrameNanos camera tickers, all JNI style calls, and Compose recomposition of one monolithic MapUiState. Neither can be parallelized further from app code — MapLibre's threading model is fixed. The only lever, and the one the entire fix list pulls, is sending each of those threads less work, and never at the moments they're already saturated. Resource utilization is not the problem; scheduling discipline at the seams is, and it's fixable.

## Confirmed findings (verified)

### 1. Nav-detached recompositions fall through to the generic whole-route fit and fire an unrequested 800ms flight
- **Where:** `app/src/main/java/app/vela/ui/map/VelaMapView.kt:1830` - severity high, verified impact medium
- **Mechanism:** The camera when-block runs in the AndroidView update lambda on every recomposition. During nav, the `navMode && myLocation != null && navFollowing` branch intentionally swallows the fall-through — but only while navFollowing is true. The moment the camera detaches (Overview tap or a pan via onNavPanned), that branch stops matching and evaluation falls to the route-fit branch, whose key is `routePolyline.hashCode()*31 + cameraBottomInsetPx*7 + cameraTopInsetPx`. lastFittedRouteKey was last written during route planning with the chooser's bottom inset; in nav the inset is different, so the key mismatches and `animateCamera(newLatLngBounds(...), 800)` fires. Interleavings: (a) user pans mid-drive to look at something → the very next recomposition (a speedo tick, a banner update — they arrive ~1Hz) launches a whole-route zoom-out flight that yanks the camera away from where they panned; (b) while detached, a reroute/faster-route swap changes routePolyline → a fresh key mismatch → another uninvited whole-route flight mid-drive; (c) on the Overview tap itself, this branch races the navOverviewTick LaunchedEffect — two flights with different paddings are issued in the same frame, and whichever runs second (order between AndroidView.update and a relaunched LaunchedEffect is not deterministic) wins, with the loser cancelled mid-flight — one source of the 'not consistently' hitchy overview transition.
- **Fix:** Gate the route-fit branch on `!navMode` (the in-nav overview already has its own dedicated fit via navOverviewTick). Optionally also update lastFittedRouteKey when navMode is true so exiting nav doesn't immediately re-fit.
- **Fix risk (from verification):** Gating the route-fit branch on `!navMode` makes the when-block fall PAST it one detach earlier, exposing the branches below during detached nav: the frameMarkers fit (fine — it is meant to frame in-nav search-along-route results) and the final else branch (VelaMapView.kt:1903-1913), which fires `animateCamera(newLatLngZoom(center, 14.5))` whenever `cameraTarget != lastCameraTarget`. That else branch is already reachable today after the key is consumed, so the exposure is not new — but if state.center updates during nav pans, the fix could trade the one-shot route flight for a zoom-14.5 recenter on the first detached recomposition. Safer variant: keep the branch matched during nav but make its body a no-op swallow (condition `routePolyline.size >= 2 && (navMode || key != lastFittedRouteKey)`), mirroring the existing swallow pattern. Also, gating on !navMode removes the accidental whole-route framing at nav start when myLocation is still null (GPS not yet acquired, branch 1790 fails) — minor, arguably desirable, but a behavior change. The optional 'update lastFittedRouteKey during nav' part is near-moot: stopNav clears activeRoute so routePolyline is empty on nav exit and the branch can't fire anyway.

### 2. Re-center from route overview snaps tilt (0→55) and top padding (0→0.45×height) in a single frame instead of gliding
- **Where:** `app/src/main/java/app/vela/ui/map/VelaMapView.kt:1054` - severity high, verified impact high
- **Mechanism:** recenterNav() only flips navCameraDetached=false; the follow ticker re-attaches via its `camState[0].isNaN()` seed block. That block seeds target, bearing and zoom from the live camera (the overview's north-up, flat, zoomed-out, zero-padding camera) so those glide — but (1) tilt is NOT seeded: navTiltEase[0] still holds 55 from before the overview (the detached else-branch at line 1095 resets only camState), so the first `moveCamera` sets tilt 55 while the camera is at 0 — a one-frame 55-degree pitch snap; (2) the puck-low `.padding(0.0, cam.height*0.45, 0.0, 0.0)` is baked verbatim into every follow frame's CameraPosition, and the overview explicitly zeroed padding — so the rendered centre jumps ~22% of screen height on the first re-attach frame. On top of that, zoom eases from the overview level (~z11) to ~z17.5 with tau 0.12s (95% in ~0.36s) — an exponential ease whose velocity is maximal at t=0, so the whole transition reads as a violent jolt rather than Google's eased fly-back. This is exactly the reported 'animating from route-overview back into nav follow hitches'.
- **Fix:** In the camState re-seed block, also seed navTiltEase[0] = cp.tilt and introduce an eased padding scalar (ease 0→0.45 with the same k, applied as fraction*cam.height) — or run a one-shot ~600ms animateCamera to the follow pose and only start the per-frame ticker when it lands. Consider a longer time-constant (or a critically-damped spring) for the re-attach zoom/position ease so the fly-back has an ease-in.
- **Fix risk (from verification):** Seeding navTiltEase[0]=cp.tilt in the re-seed block is low-risk but interacts with the shove-set navUserTilt override and the north-up toggle (seed from a transient mid-gesture tilt would briefly ease from an odd value). An eased padding scalar must (a) be reset in the detached else-branch alongside camState or a mid-transition detach/re-attach resumes from a stale fraction, (b) terminate exactly at 0.45 or the puck-low framing drifts permanently (padding is sticky MapLibre state, torn down at nav end at lines 969-977), and (c) not leak into the pre-engage padding path. The one-shot animateCamera alternative is riskier: the per-frame ticker's moveCamera cancels animateCamera, so the ticker must genuinely idle during the flight, and the puck keeps advancing on GPS during the ~600 ms so the animation lands on a stale pose needing a second seam. A longer zoom time-constant must be scoped to re-attach only — loosening the shared k=0.12 s would lag normal follow tracking through turns.

### 3. Free-drive ticker's cold-seed moveCamera kills the locate/launch flight: recenter from a zoomed-out or panned-away view teleports instead of flying
- **Where:** `app/src/main/java/app/vela/ui/map/VelaMapView.kt:788` - severity high, verified impact medium
- **Mechanism:** onRecenter sets followMe=true before vm.recenter(), so driveFollowing flips true and the free-drive ticker relaunches in the same recomposition that the launch-centre effect (line 940) or the recenterTick browseZoomGoal path handles. The ticker's first frame runs the browseCam NaN seed: if camera zoom < 14 it calls `moveCamera(newLatLngZoom(loc, 15.5))` — an instant snap that also cancels the in-flight `animateCamera(..., 650)` from the once-per-session centre (line 940) or any locate animation; even when zoom >= 14, browseCam seeds from the CURRENT camera target (potentially kilometres from the fix after a pan) and eases toward it with tau 0.22s — an exponential whose velocity peaks at t=0, crossing multi-km in under a second with no ease-in and no zoom-out-and-in arc, while tile fetch + symbol placement (which re-runs on the render thread during camera motion) can't keep up. Net: the locate tap either teleports (reads as a dropped animation) or does a violent flat whoosh — the reported 'flights hitch after a locate tap'. The 650ms launch glide can never survive because the ticker's per-frame moveCamera fires on frame one (lastBrowse is NaN so `moved` is true).
- **Fix:** When follow re-arms far from the fix (or from zoom < 14), don't seed-and-snap in the ticker: run one animateCamera flight to (fix, 15.5) with the ticker holding off (e.g. a `flyingIn` flag set before followMe re-arms, cleared in the animation's onFinish, seeding browseCam from the landing camera). Alternatively have onRecenter defer followMe=true until the flight finishes and let the recenterTick animateCamera(500) branch run it.
- **Fix risk (from verification):** The cold-seed snap exists to fix a real prior bug ("came back zoomed to the whole US" after crash relaunch, comment at :782-786) — a flight-with-ticker-holdoff must still guarantee the camera lands at street zoom even if the animation is cancelled (gesture mid-flight, activity pause), or that regression returns. MapLibre's animateCamera cancel/finish callbacks are the coordination point: if flyingIn is never cleared on a cancelled flight, the ticker stays parked and follow goes dead until the next state flip; if a gesture during the flight doesn't drop followMe AND clear the flag, the flight and the user fight. Deferring followMe=true until onFinish changes driveFollowing timing that other consumers key off (scale-bar hiding, the effect-reset that NaNs browseBeam/browseCam, speed-overlay comments warn about style churn tied to this flag). Seeding browseCam from the landing camera must also tolerate the fix having moved during the flight (driving), or the first post-flight frame jumps. Finally the launch-centre effect (:929) and the new recenter flight can double-fire on cold start — one must yield or two flights race.

### 4. Nav teardown effect is re-keyed on routePolyline: every route change while browsing snaps the camera and wipes the sheet inset padding
- **Where:** `app/src/main/java/app/vela/ui/map/VelaMapView.kt:956` - severity medium, verified impact medium
- **Mechanism:** LaunchedEffect(navMode, routePolyline) relaunches on ANY key change, and its !navMode branch unconditionally executes `moveCamera(bearing 0, tilt 0, padding(0,0,0,0))`. With navMode false, every routePolyline identity change — the directions fetch landing, picking an alternate in the chooser, a long-press add-stop refetch — re-runs that snap. Two effects: (a) it races the route-fit animateCamera(800) the when-block fires for the same new polyline in the same frame; LaunchedEffect bodies dispatch on the UI frame dispatcher while AndroidView.update runs at composition commit, so ordering varies — when the moveCamera lands second it cancels the fit on frame ~0 and the map just freezes at the old framing (an intermittent 'route didn't frame' hitch, matching 'not consistently laggy'); (b) the padding(0,0,0,0) silently wipes the `map.setPadding(0,0,0,cameraBottomInsetPx)` the inset block set for the chooser, and since lastInsetPx isn't invalidated, padding stays wrong until the inset VALUE next changes — subsequent camera targets centre behind the sheet.
- **Fix:** Only run the teardown on an actual nav-exit transition: track the previous navMode (e.g. a remembered wasNav flag) and execute the reset moveCamera solely when wasNav && !navMode; the routePolyline key can stay for the ticker relaunch. Also re-apply the current inset (or set lastInsetPx = -1) after the reset so padding stays coherent.
- **Fix risk (from verification):** Gating the reset on a wasNav→!navMode transition is sound but has three edges: (a) on a GENUINE nav exit the padding zero is still needed and still wipes a sheet inset if one is open — the suggested `lastInsetPx = -1` re-arm makes the update block's `grew` check true, which nulls `lastCameraTarget` and can fire an unexpected re-frame of a stale target right after ending a drive (that null-on-grow was itself a deliberate 2026-07-09 fix, lines 1722-1731); re-applying the current inset directly is safer. (b) The unconditional snap incidentally levels a stale rotated/tilted browse camera on every route change; with it gated, a rotated camera (rotation done while a sheet suppressed free-drive follow) persists into the plain `newLatLngBounds` fit, which keeps current bearing — a tilted/rotated route overview could reappear (the exact class of bug the 2026-07-15 nav-overview comment at lines 907-911 describes). (c) The wasNav flag must survive recomposition but reset sanely on process recreation mid-nav; and routePolyline must STAY a key so mid-nav reroutes still relaunch the puck ticker (the fix as suggested preserves this).

### 5. applyData tail mutates styles unconditionally on every recomposition (alt routes, me-source, me/arrow layer props, preview pin, route gradient)
- **Where:** `app/src/main/java/app/vela/ui/map/VelaMapView.kt:3631` - severity high, verified impact medium
- **Mechanism:** The top half of applyData is identity-gated, but everything from line 3631 down runs on EVERY recomposition: (1) altFc is rebuilt (up to 4 polylines x hundreds-to-thousands of vertices mapped to Point objects) and ALT_ROUTE_SRC.setGeoJson + ALT_ROUTE_LAYER.setProperties(lineColor) fire unconditionally -- even an unchanged or EMPTY alternates list re-uploads and re-tessellates the source; (2) the !navMode route branch re-sets ROUTE_LAYER lineGradient (a fresh Expression, forcing a 256-texel gradient rebake) plus 3-4 visibility setProperties every pass; (3) ME_SRC.setGeoJson (line 3798) re-uploads the location point per recomposition in browse; (4) ME_LAYER + ME_ARROW_LAYER setProperties (lines 3806-3813) and PREVIEW_SRC.setGeoJson (line 3820) run every pass. applyData is invoked from the AndroidView update lambda (lines 1701/1709), i.e. on every VelaMapView recomposition: each ambient partial paint, each scale-bar 1% tick during a pinch, each nav speedo/fix tick, each sheet state flip. Every setGeoJson/setProperties is render-thread work that invalidates sources/layers and re-runs symbol placement -- exactly while an animateCamera flight (locate tap, route-overview -> nav follow) or a pan gesture owns the render thread. With the chooser open (alternates present) each recomposition re-tessellates the full alternate geometry; during flights the empty-but-unconditional uploads still force placement invalidation mid-flight, which is the visible hitch.
- **Fix:** Extend the existing identity-gate pattern to the whole tail: gate alternates on `alternates !== lastAppliedAlternates` (plus altColor), gate the ME_SRC upload on (me, bearing) actually changing, gate ME_LAYER/ME_ARROW_LAYER setProperties on the derived (showPuck, meStale, navMode) tuple changing, gate PREVIEW_SRC on preview != lastPreviewTarget, and gate the browse-route lineGradient on (p, routeInt, trafficSpans) changing. All are one-field private vars, same shape as lastAppliedMarkers.
- **Fix risk (from verification):** Three regression traps. (1) The style-reload callback (VelaMapView.kt:1684-1694) nulls every existing lastApplied* holder because a fresh style has empty sources; any new gate (alternates, me, me-layer tuple, preview, gradient) MUST be added to that reset list or the puck/alternates/preview pin silently vanish after a theme/palette/satellite flip. (2) ME_SRC has a documented ownership handoff with the nav and free-drive tickers (comments at 445-447, 719-722, 3785-3787): a gate keyed on (me, bearing) can go stale when the ticker was the last writer, so on a follow→browse or nav→browse transition applyData may skip re-asserting the source and leave the puck at the ticker's last eased point; the gate must be invalidated on navMode/follow transitions. (3) Gating alternates on referential identity (!==) fails if the VM re-emits structurally-equal but fresh lists (copy-on-emit StateFlow pattern) — use structural equality like the existing markers gate, which costs a list compare but never misses an update. Gating the browse lineGradient on (p, routeInt, trafficSpans) is safe since mbgl already no-ops equal values; the gate only saves JNI/allocation, so getting its key wrong (e.g. omitting trafficSpans) would stale the traffic bands on the preview route line.

### 6. Ambient POI setGeoJson (whole-layer collision re-placement) lands mid-gesture and mid-flight with no motion deferral
- **Where:** `app/src/main/java/app/vela/ui/map/VelaMapView.kt:3690` - severity high, verified impact medium
- **Mechanism:** Each ambient upload replaces the full AMBIENT_SRC FeatureCollection (up to AMBIENT_ONSCREEN_CAP = 140 features, built on the main thread inside composition with per-place groupFor/colorFor string matching), and MapLibre then re-tessellates and re-runs symbol placement for the whole layer on the render thread. The stream fan-out (GoogleMapsDataSource.nearbyPlaces, batches of 10 then 25 at >=500 ms, plus the slim-flavor heal refetch ~1.2 s later, plus the final ranked pool) means 3-6 such uploads land in the seconds after a viewport settle -- which is exactly when the user has resumed panning or when the locate-tap flight/zoom ease is still running. Placement re-runs during camera motion compound with the per-motion-frame placement MapLibre already does, so each landing is a visible frame spike in dense areas on any device. The batching/sort-key fixes reduced HOW MANY uploads happen; nothing controls WHEN they happen relative to camera motion.
- **Fix:** Defer ambient (and marker) source uploads while the camera is in motion: latch a pending FeatureCollection when gestureMove/an active animateCamera/the follow ticker is driving the camera, and flush it on the next camera-idle (the onViewport/settle listener already exists). A 100-200 ms post-idle debounce covers the flight case. Optionally move the FeatureCollection construction off the main thread (build in the VM on Dispatchers.Default and pass the ready FC down).
- **Fix risk (from verification):** Three regression surfaces. (1) Free-drive-follow starvation: the browse follow ticker drives the camera via moveCamera every frame, so a deferral keyed on 'the follow ticker is driving the camera' would hold ambient uploads indefinitely while driving on the bare map (dots never update until the car stops or follow drops). (2) First-paint latency: the streaming partial-paint pipeline exists specifically to get first dots on screen ~1s in (device-measured cold-start metric ~3.0s); a post-idle debounce plus deferral delays exactly that, and a user who pans continuously in a dense downtown could see dots noticeably later or never while browsing. (3) Latch/flush correctness: idle detection is subtle here — moveCamera cancels animateCamera without a guaranteed gesture-idle sequence, and gestureMove[0] is only true for REASON_API_GESTURE — so a pending-FC latch keyed to the wrong signal can be held forever (stale/empty ambient layer) or flush out of order, reintroducing the shrink-blink and stale-dots-after-cancel bugs the VM's never-shrink and liveness guards were added to fix. Moving FC construction off the main thread must also preserve the lastAppliedAmbient identity gate and partial ordering, or a slow build could apply an older FC over a newer one.

### 7. Search-result marker bitmaps are generated synchronously on the main thread inside the applyData marker loop, coinciding with the cluster-fit flight
- **Where:** `app/src/main/java/app/vela/ui/map/VelaMapView.kt:3654` - severity medium, verified impact low
- **Mechanism:** When a result set lands, the markers loop calls PoiIcons.ensureResultIcon per result inside composition: each NEW icon key (rating bubbles are keyed by rating tenths, fuel bubbles by price, so a 20-60 result search can need 10-30 unique bitmaps) does a Bitmap.createBitmap + antialiased Canvas path/text draw (~84x100 to bubble-width px) plus style.addImage, which is itself a render-thread style mutation that can trigger sprite-atlas rebuilds. groupFor (keyword scan) also runs twice per marker (resultIconKey + resultBubbleLabel). This burst executes on the UI thread in the same frame window where the marker-fit animateCamera starts framing the cluster, so the flight's first frames are starved -- one mechanism behind 'camera flights hitch'.
- **Fix:** Pre-generate the bitmaps off the main thread: when results land in the VM, render the needed icon bitmaps on Dispatchers.Default keyed by iconKey, then hand applyData a ready map so the loop only calls style.addImage for keys the style lacks. Alternatively cache generated Bitmaps in PoiIcons across styles (keyed by key+dark+satellite) so only the first-ever search pays the draw cost.
- **Fix risk (from verification):** Pre-generating off-main-thread introduces a readiness race: markers can reach applyData before their bitmaps do, and a SymbolLayer icon referencing a missing style image renders the pin invisible — the fix needs a synchronous fallback ensure (keeping the current path) or a repaint when bitmaps land. - **Fix risk (from verification):** The free-drive follow ticker issues per-frame moveCamera while driveFollowing (a default-on bare-map browse state), so a generic isCameraAnimating/move-started gate would read 'always moving' and starve ambient paints for the entire follow glide (bare map, dots never appear/refresh mid-drive). A missed idle/cancel event (moveCamera cancels animateCamera; MapLibre cancel callbacks are easy to drop) could hold queued paints indefinitely. The hold must be scoped to programmatic animateCamera flights, carry a timeout + flush-on-cancel, and still let the final ranked pool land promptly, else the fix trades an intermittent hitch for missing/stale POI dots — a worse, more visible regression. Also note fixing this will not cure the route-overview→nav-follow hitch (different cause), so verify on-device before closing the user report.

### 20. Nav route-split re-uploads the entire remaining polyline suffix every 150 ms at speed
- **Where:** `app/src/main/java/app/vela/ui/map/VelaMapView.kt:1120` - severity medium, verified impact medium
- **Mechanism:** The nav ticker's driven/ahead geometry split (1108-1145) rebuilds an ArrayList of every remaining route point and setGeoJson's it (plus two lineGradient setProperties) whenever the puck moves ~1-3 m, floor-throttled to 150 ms. On a long trip that is a several-thousand-point LineString tessellated on the render thread up to ~6x/s for the whole drive, and it keeps running during the route-overview flight (899-920) and the overview->follow re-attach ease — the exact moments the user reports hitching. The upload size never shrinks meaningfully until the drive is nearly done (the suffix IS the remaining route).
- **Fix:** Bound the re-uploaded geometry: keep the far tail as a static second feature uploaded once per route/reroute, and re-upload only the leading window (e.g. the next 2-5 km, zoom-scaled) around the cut point every 150 ms; or decimate the suffix with a zoom-scaled Douglas-Peucker before upload (the cut point needs metre precision, the tail does not).
- **Fix risk (from verification):** Splitting into a static tail + leading window breaks lineGradient parameterization: line-progress is per-feature, so the traffic-span remap (1128-1134) and the gradient restart at the seam — spans must be re-split per feature (or the tail gets its own layer/gradient) or congestion colors will be wrong across the boundary. Other risks: a visible seam (cap/join doubling or a 1px gap) at the window/tail junction at street zoom; stale-tail bugs if the once-per-route tail upload misses a reroute/faster-route swap or the puck crossing the window edge (wrong route drawn ahead, the exact class of bug the hermetic-replay work fixed); Douglas-Peucker decimation changes cumulative suffix length so fraction-mapped traffic spans shift slightly and the crisp cut-point requirement means the leading segment must stay exact; and the overview flight shows the whole route, so an aggressively decimated tail reads chunky exactly when the user is looking at it. Also note the repo's own doc warns the geometry split exists because gradients cannot render a crisp cut — any fix must keep the metre-precise cut point in the re-uploaded leading feature.

### 21. Everything time-critical serializes on two single threads: the main thread (tickers + all JNI style calls + recomposition) and the one MapLibre render thread
- **Where:** `app/src/main/java/app/vela/ui/map/VelaMapView.kt:731` - severity low, verified impact medium
- **Mechanism:** Both per-frame tickers (free-drive at 720-893, nav puck at 956-1148) run via withFrameNanos on the main thread, issuing moveCamera + setMeSource JNI calls at 60 Hz; the same thread hosts Compose recomposition of the monolithic MapUiState and the AndroidView update block. Any main-thread stall (a heavy recomposition, GC from the ~180 MB/12 s pan churn already documented in CLAUDE.md, a list allocation storm from finding 2) skips ticker frames and shows as a camera stutter even when the GL thread is idle. On the other side, MapLibre's single render thread serializes symbol placement, source uploads and drawing — the C++ tile workers parallelize decode/tessellation, but placement and every setGeoJson/setProperties applied from this codebase queue behind the frame. Neither thread's work can be parallelized further from app code; the only lever is sending less to each (findings 1-4). For the record: dispatchers are used correctly (GoogleMapsDataSource wraps all network+parse in Dispatchers.IO via io() at GoogleMapsDataSource.kt:641 with a parallel async fan-out at :172-174; rank counting on Dispatchers.Default at MapViewModel.kt:2880), no code forces software rendering or reads pixels back on the phone path (MapSnapshotter is Android-Auto-only), there are no manual invalidate()/requestRender() calls, and the ~50-setProperties applyLight/applyDark passes run only inside the one-shot style-load callback (VelaMapView.kt:1698) — a palette swap is a full style reload, not a mutation storm; the nav declutter toggle is ~7 one-shot calls (437-463).
- **Fix:** No re-threading needed — keep the model, shrink the per-frame payload: land findings 1-4, and consider moving the me-source upload to a small property-only update (setGeoJson with a reused Feature) plus batching the ticker's two style writes per frame into one.
- **Fix risk (from verification):** The specific suggested fix is near-no-op and partly incoherent: setMeSource already uploads a single one-point Feature (reusing the Feature object saves one tiny allocation, not the render-thread upload cost), and the ticker's 'two style writes' cannot be batched into one — setGeoJson is a source upload and moveCamera a camera transform, different native entry points. Attempting to merge or reorder them risks intra-frame puck/camera desync (the ticker deliberately draws the puck at the eased camera position — the documented 'dot teleport' bug class), and touching setMeSource ownership risks regressing the mePaint/browseBeam ownership contract that applyData respects. The safe remediation is the already-planned 'send less' work in findings 1-4, not re-threading or write-batching.
